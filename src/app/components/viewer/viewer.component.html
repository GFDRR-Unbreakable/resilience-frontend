About Unbreakable

Economic losses from natural disasters totaled $210 billion in 2016. Such statements, while true, imply that the impact of natural disasters is limited to the damage inflicted on buildings, infrastructure, and agricultural production. However, $1 in losses does not mean the same thing to a rich person as it does to a poor person; the gravity of any loss depends on whom it affects, and the traditional focus on aggregate losses obscures the plight of poor people by restricting consideration to how natural disasters affect those wealthy enough to have considerable assets to lose in the first place.

In 2016, the World Bank released Unbreakable, a flagship report that goes beyond traditional metrics like gross asset losses to examine how natural disasters affect people’s well-being. In an average year, the poorest 20% of people in a country suffer less than 10% of aggregate asset losses, but fully 25% of aggregate well-being losses. In other words, poor people suffer only a fraction of gross economic losses caused by natural disasters, but they bear a much larger brunt of natural disasters’ consequences. This approach takes fuller account of how poverty increases people’s vulnerability to natural disasters, and how natural disasters trap people in poverty.
